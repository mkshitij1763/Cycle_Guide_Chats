----------------------------------------

[02:54 pm] Coach:
    Your fertile window doesn’t start only when LH peaks. It actually begins a bit earlier, when estrogen starts rising from your baseline, which is why Inito has marked High Fertility on CD 7.
    
    After your period ends, your body slowly starts preparing for ovulation. As a follicle grows, estrogen levels rise, this helps thicken the uterine lining and signals that ovulation may be approaching. Since your estrogen has risen above your baseline, Inito identified that you’re entering your fertile window.
    
    Inito also considers your average cycle length and past cycle data when giving fertility ratings, which helps anticipate when ovulation is likely to happen. As estrogen continues to rise, it eventually triggers the release of LH. When LH rises from your baseline, Inito then marks Peak Fertility.
    
    So being in your High Fertility now simply means your body is gearing up for ovulation. Inito will continue to look for a rise in your LH from your baseline and mark your Peak Fertility at that time, so make sure to continue testing!

    Inito marks High Fertility when there is a rise in estrogen (E3G) from your baseline levels, and Peak Fertility when there is a rise in LH from your baseline. Sometimes estrogen can rise earlier in the cycle, but LH may take a little longer to surge.     On your chart, your LH rose above baseline on CD 15, which is when Inito marked Peak Fertility. After Peak Fertility, you may still see one more day of High Fertility. This is because although ovulation usually happens about 24–36 hours after the LH surge, the egg can survive for about 12–24 hours after it’s released, so the possibility of conception can still exist during that time. That’s why Inito marks the day after Peak Fertility as High Fertility as well.
    
    So the multiple High Fertility days you’re seeing happened because estrogen had already risen earlier while Inito was still waiting to detect your LH surge, which eventually occurred on CD 15, followed by another day of High Fertility.
