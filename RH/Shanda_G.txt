--- TUESDAY, 10 FEBRUARY 2026 ---

[12:13 pm] Coach:
    Hey! Yes, this can happen sometimes. A shift from low fertility to peak fertility usually means your LH rose significantly from your baseline, which is why Inito marked it as Peak Fertility. Also, based on the average of your cycle length, you may experience another LH surge later if this first one didn’t trigger ovulation. Continuing to test will help confirm whether this LH rise is followed by a steady rise in PdG, as that’s what confirms ovulation.
    Also, just a heads up, hormones can fluctuate, so ovulation doesn’t always happen on the same cycle day each month. Sometimes your body may prepare for ovulation a little earlier or take longer in some cycles. Hormones are also influenced by lifestyle factors like diet, stress, and activity levels, so some variation is expected.
    If you’d like to know more, this blog might be useful: https://blog.inito.com/do-you-ovulate-the-same-day-every-month/
