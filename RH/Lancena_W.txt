[11:38 pm] Coach:
    Hey Lancena! Here’s the catch, the LH molecule is made up of two subunits: alpha and beta. The difference you’re seeing could be because OPKs like Clearblue measure alpha LH, which has a higher chance of cross-reacting with other hormones like FSH, TSH, and hCG.
    
    In contrast, Inito measures the beta LH. The beta subunit is different for each of these hormones, which gives beta LH an added advantage in tracking your ovulation with higher accuracy since there is a lower chance of cross-reactivity.
    
    Looking at your chart, your LH has started rising a little. It’s possible that it may continue to rise tomorrow. However, if you tend to have a rapid surge, there’s also a chance that it could be missed. If you’d like, you can test twice today to better capture the surge. 💛
