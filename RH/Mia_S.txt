[03:00 pm] Coach:
    After stopping birth control, it can take a few weeks or even a few months for your cycle to settle back into its natural rhythm. Tracking your hormones during this time can be helpful, but it’s also normal for things to look a bit irregular in the first few cycles.
    
    Sporadic testing after ovulation can sometimes make hormone patterns feel confusing, since hormones naturally fluctuate. Seeing an estrogen value in the upper trend later in the cycle can happen as well, since estrogen is released in pulses and levels naturally fluctuate. PdG fluctuations during the luteal phase are also expected.
    
    Hormone levels are influenced by lifestyle factors like stress, diet, sleep, and activity, so some variations are typical. What’s most helpful is focusing on overall trends across cycles rather than individual values.
