[12:01 am] Coach:
    Hey! LH is made up of two subunits: Alpha LH and Beta LH.
    
    The Alpha subunit is similar across several hormones in the body, which means it can sometimes react with hormones like FSH, TSH, or hCG. Because of this, Alpha LH has a higher chance of cross-reactivity.
    
    The Beta LH subunit is unique in structure and is more hormone-specific, which is why Inito focuses on measuring Beta LH. This helps provide a clearer and more precise view of your true LH surge.
    
    That said, in some cycles—especially when LH peaks are smaller—looking at both Alpha and Beta LH together can offer a fuller understanding of your hormone pattern and help ensure subtle surges aren’t missed. That’s why dual strips can be useful in certain cases.
    
    If you’d like to read more about this, this blog might be helpful:
    https://blog.inito.com/alpha-lh-vs-beta-lh/
