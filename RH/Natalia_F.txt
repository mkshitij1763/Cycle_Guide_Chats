[07:16 pm] Coach:
    Hey Natalia! Looking at your chart, your LH has continued to rise today, which suggests a gradual LH surge pattern, which around 57% of women experience. Inito marks Peak Fertility when it detects a rise in LH from your baseline. Your LH can continue rising after that before it reaches its highest point and then drop, that highest point is the LH peak.
    
    LH surges can look different from person to person and even from cycle to cycle. For ease of calculation, ovulation day is marked as the day after Peak Fertility, provided ovulation is later confirmed with a steady PdG rise. Ovulation occurs about 24–36 hours after the LH rise and 8–20 hours after the LH peak, irrespective of the type of surge. Keep testing so you can capture the full LH trend and confirm ovulation with PdG 💛
    
    To help clarify the difference between LH rise, LH surge, and LH peak, this guide breaks it down really well: https://blog.inito.com/lh-rise-vs-lh-surge-vs-peak-fertility/
