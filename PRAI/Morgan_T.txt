[07:59 pm] Coach:
    Great question — this actually trips up a lot of people, so you’re not alone 💛
    
    FSH doesn’t rise in a straight line throughout the cycle. Early on, it often bumps up briefly to help recruit a group of follicles, and then it drops back down. That drop is intentional.
    
    Here’s what’s happening biologically:
    👉🏼 Your brain sends out FSH to wake up multiple follicles
    👉🏼 As one follicle starts responding better than the others, it makes estrogen
    👉🏼 That estrogen signals back to the brain: “I’ve got this”
    👉🏼 In response, the brain dials FSH down, so only the strongest follicle keeps growing
    
    So seeing FSH come down after an early rise doesn’t mean you’re moving away from ovulation — it usually means your body is narrowing in on a lead follicle.
    
    FSH can also show a small rise again near your LH surge (this happens in about half of cycles), but it’s not required for ovulation to happen.
