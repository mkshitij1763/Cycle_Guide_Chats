    Ovulation confirmation is not decided based on how many days you are past Peak Fertility.
    It is decided based on pattern, not a single number.
    
    With urine PdG monitoring:
    
    • Ovulation is typically confirmed 3 to 7 days after the LH surge
    • We look for a sustained rise above your personal baseline
    • Not just one spike, but consistency for multiple days
    
    If PdG does not show a sustained rise within about 7–10 days after the LH surge, that’s when we start considering:
    
    • Delayed ovulation
    • An anovulatory cycle
    • Or occasionally a very weak luteal progesterone response
    
    But even then, one cycle alone does not diagnose anything.
    
    There is no universal PdG number that defines weak ovulation for everyone. What matters more is:
    
    • Did PdG clearly rise from your follicular baseline?
    • Did it remain elevated for several days?
    • Did it stay elevated for at least 7–10 days before dropping?
    
    Some perfectly healthy cycles have modest PdG rises.
    Some strong ovulations show dramatic rises.
    Both can be common.
    
    🌺 If you’re worried your PdG levels rose late right now
    
    A few important things to remember:
    
    👉🏻 Progesterone release is pulsatile. It fluctuates hour to hour.
    👉🏻 Urine concentration changes with hydration.
    👉🏻 Ovulation day can be off by 1–2 days from the predicted peak.
    👉🏻 PdG sometimes rises gradually, not dramatically.
