    how high should LH get during a peakV
----------------------------------------

[06:39 pm] Coach:
    There isn’t one specific LH number that defines “peak.”
    
    Here’s what matters:
    
    🌼 It’s about the rise, not the number
    
    LH is pulsatile and highly individual. Research shows that LH levels during a surge can vary widely from person to person. While one study observed an average surge level around 44.6 mIU/mL, ovulation has been documented at levels as low as 6.5 mIU/mL and as high as 101 mIU/mL.
    So even if your LH reading seems lower than expected, ovulation can still occur. What we’re looking for is a clear upward shift from your baseline, followed by the typical sequence that supports ovulation. It’s the pattern — not a specific number — that matters most.
    The absolute value is less important than a clear upward shift from your baseline pattern.
    
    🌻 What defines a true surge pattern
    • A noticeable jump compared to your previous few days
    • A distinct peak followed by a fall
    • Ideally followed by a PdG rise in the days after
    
    That sequence (rise → peak → fall → PdG rise) is what confirms ovulation timing — not hitting a specific number.
    
    🌺 A few important nuances
    • Some cycles have strong, sharp surges
    • Some have plateau-type surges
    • Some people experience two ore more LH rises before ovulation
    • Hydration and time of testing can influence the measured value
    
    So instead of asking “How high should it go?” the better question is:
    👉 “Did it clearly shift from my baseline and follow the ovulatory sequence?”
