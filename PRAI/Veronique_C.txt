[09:41 am] Veronique C:
    How do you confirm ovulation??
----------------------------------------

[09:57 am] Coach:
    Inito confirms ovulation by looking for a sustained rise in PdG (a urine marker of progesterone) after your fertile window.
    Here’s how it works step by step:
    • Estrogen rises first as a follicle grows
    • LH rises next to signal that ovulation is likely to happen
    • PdG rises only after ovulation has actually occurred
    Inito waits to see multiple days of elevated PdG because progesterone doesn’t rise immediately. It usually increases a few days after ovulation and peaks at 6 to 8 DPO. This sustained rise is what confirms ovulation.
    This is why:
    • LH helps predict ovulation
    • PdG helps confirm ovulation
    • Confirmation always happens in hindsight, not the same day
    Inito doesn’t assess “normal” or “abnormal” hormone levels. It focuses on patterns and trends over time, which is why confirmation may take a few days to appear.
----------------------------------------

[09:58 am] Veronique C:
    Why if it appears faster though?
----------------------------------------

[10:59 am] Coach:
    Good question — this is something many people notice.
    Ovulation confirmation can sometimes appear faster because of how progesterone (PdG) shows up in urine and how your body responds after ovulation. A few common reasons:
    • Progesterone production can ramp up quickly
    In some cycles, the corpus luteum starts producing progesterone soon after ovulation, so PdG shows a noticeable rise earlier than average.
    • Your baseline that cycle is lower or clearer
    If your pre-ovulation PdG baseline is well defined, even an early rise stands out more clearly, allowing Inito to detect the shift sooner.
    • Strong ovulatory response
    A well-timed LH surge followed by a strong luteal response can lead to a quicker PdG increase. This doesn’t mean anything is wrong — it’s just how that cycle unfolded.
    • Testing timing and hydration
    Urine concentration and test timing can make PdG appear to rise earlier on the chart, even though the underlying biology is still following the same sequence.
    The key thing to remember:
    👉 Faster confirmation doesn’t mean ovulation happened earlier than expected
    👉 It just means the progesterone signal became clear sooner
    Inito is still looking for the same thing every time — a sustained upward PdG trend. Whether that trend appears sooner or later depends on the cycle, not on an error.
    So variation in confirmation timing is normal. What matters is that the pattern completes, not how fast it shows up.
