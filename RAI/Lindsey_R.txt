    Here’s how to read your cycles simply:
    Focus on trends, not numbers
    Your hormone values can vary cycle to cycle, but the pattern is what matters
    
    👉🏻 E3G (estrogen)
    Look for a steady rise before your ovulation marking the start of your fertile window → this is your body preparing for ovulation
    
    👉🏻 LH
    Watch for your surge pattern → ovulation usually follows soon after (24 to 36 hours after your LH surge)

    After ovulation, you’re looking for a sustained rise pattern → this confirms ovulation
    It can fluctuate day to day since progesterone is released in pulses
    
    Over multiple cycles, notice:
    • When your LH surge usually happens
    • If ovulation gets confirmed each cycle
    • How long your luteal phase feels
    • Whether your patterns are repeating
    
    💡 Your goal isn’t “perfect hormones”
    It’s consistent patterns that help you time and understand ovulation better.

[11:47 pm] Coach:
    🌷 Why your LH surge looks lower 
    
    1️⃣ Ovulation depends on the rise, not the absolute number
    Inito identifies ovulation by detecting a rise from your personal baseline, not by reaching a universal threshold.
    LH levels starting from 6.5 to 101 mIU/ml can cause ovulation. Studies show that average LH levels during a surge are 44.6 mIU/ml.  https://www.researchgate.net/publication/272175944_Development_of_the_first_urinary_reproductive_hormone_ranges_referenced_to_independently_determined_ovulation_day
    
    2️⃣ Urinary LH levels vary widely
    LH in urine is influenced by:
    
    • hydration status
    • time of testing
    • metabolism & clearance rate
    • kidney filtration differences
    • baseline hormone physiology
    
    So even if you are ovulating at the same time every cycle, you can see very different numbers.
    
    3️⃣ Blood LH ≠ urine LH
    Urinary LH reflects what your body excretes, not the exact blood concentration.
    
    Some women produce a sharp, tall spike.
    Others produce a shorter or gentler surge that still triggers ovulation.
    
    4️⃣ Surge duration matters more than height
    A surge can be:
    
    • sharp & tall
    • short & narrow
    • gradual & modest
    
    Ovulation occurs if the brain-ovary signal is sufficient, regardless of peak height.
    
    What matters most:
    
    ✔ LH rise present
    ✔ PdG rise afterward
    
    Continue testing to look for a steady and consistent rise in PdG to confirm ovulation.
