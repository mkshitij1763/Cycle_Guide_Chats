[09:48 pm] Ellie F:
    Is it normal to have a cycle that is 27 days long and one that is 31 days long
----------------------------------------

[10:18 pm] Coach:
    Yes
    
    Menstrual cycles aren’t meant to run like clockwork. For many people, a variation of a few days from cycle to cycle is expected.
    
    🌿 What’s considered normal
    Typical cycle length: 21–35 days
    Normal variation: up to ~7–9 days between cycles
    So a 27-day cycle followed by a 31-day cycle is well within the normal range.
    
    🌸 Why cycle length can vary
    Small shifts usually happen because ovulation doesn’t occur on the exact same day every month. This can be influenced by:
    👉🏻 stress or travel
    👉🏻 illness or inflammation
    👉🏻 sleep changes
    👉🏻 weight or exercise changes
    👉🏻 emotional stress
    👉🏻 hormonal fluctuations
    👉🏻 cysts or delayed follicle development
    
    Even subtle lifestyle changes can nudge ovulation later, which lengthens the cycle.
    
    🌼 When to look closer
    Variation may need evaluation if:
    👉🏻 cycles vary more than ~10 days regularly
    👉🏻 cycles are consistently <21 or >35 days
    👉🏻 ovulation is frequently not confirmed
    👉🏻 luteal phase is consistently <10 days
    With hormone tracking, it’s common to see these natural shifts more clearly, because ovulation timing — not your period — is what truly sets cycle length.
    
    
    🌸Related Inito resources 
    https://blog.inito.com/periods-date-change-every-month/
