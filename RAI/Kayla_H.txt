[03:54 pm] Coach:
    Let’s unpack this calmly and scientifically, because hormone charts are more like fingerprints than report cards. No two look alike, and “higher” does not mean “better.” 🌿
    
    🌷 Why your LH surge looks lower than others
    
    1️⃣ Ovulation depends on the rise, not the absolute number
    Inito identifies ovulation by detecting a rise from your personal baseline, not by reaching a universal threshold.
    LH levels starting from 6.5 to 101 mIU/ml can cause ovulation. Studies show that average LH levels during a surge are 44.6 mIU/ml.  https://www.researchgate.net/publication/272175944_Development_of_the_first_urinary_reproductive_hormone_ranges_referenced_to_independently_determined_ovulation_day
    
    2️⃣ Urinary LH levels vary widely between individuals
    LH in urine is influenced by:
    
    • hydration status
    • time of testing
    • metabolism & clearance rate
    • kidney filtration differences
    • baseline hormone physiology
    
    So two women ovulating at the same time can show very different numbers.
    
    3️⃣ Blood LH ≠ urine LH
    Urinary LH reflects what your body excretes, not the exact blood concentration.
    
    Some women produce a sharp, tall spike.
    Others produce a shorter or gentler surge that still triggers ovulation.
    
    4️⃣ Surge duration matters more than height
    A surge can be:
    
    • sharp & tall
    • short & narrow
    • gradual & modest
    
    Ovulation occurs if the brain-ovary signal is sufficient, regardless of peak height.
    
    5️⃣ Your chart confirms ovulation
    What matters most:
    
    ✔ LH rise present
    ✔ PdG rise afterward
