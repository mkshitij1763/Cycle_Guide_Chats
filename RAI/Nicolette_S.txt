    Hi Nicolette, thank you for sharing that. Sorry for your loss. Trying again after a loss takes a quiet kind of courage. I’m glad you’re looking closely at your charts and your body’s signals. Let’s walk through how ovulation is identified using your recent cycle.
    
    Inito confirms ovulation by tracking the natural hormone sequence your body follows.
    
    First, it detects a rise in Estrogen which marks the start of your fertile window. Then a surge in LH, which signals that ovulation may occur within the next 24–36 hours. 
    
    After ovulation, the follicle becomes the corpus luteum and produces progesterone. Progesterone is broken down into PdG, which appears in urine. Inito looks for a sustained rise in PdG after the LH surge to confirm that ovulation actually occurred.
    
    For ease of counting, Inito marks the day after your Peak Fertility as your Ovulation day, provided your PdG has risen steadily and consistently after the latest LH surge.

    🌼 Spotting is light bleeding (pink/brown, minimal, may not need a pad)
    
    🌺 Your period starts when you see a steady red flow that needs a pad/tampon
    
    🌻 Think of spotting as your body preparing, and full flow as Day 1
    
    🌸 For tracking, always mark Day 1 as the first day of full flow, not spotting
