[09:13 am] Meghan G:
    What does brown spotting or discharge before a period mean?
----------------------------------------

[09:27 am] Coach:
    Brown spotting before a period is quite common. It’s usually older blood leaving the uterus as hormone levels shift at the end of the cycle, and for many people a period can start with a day or two of brown discharge before full flow begins.
    Since you’re trying to conceive, light spotting earlier in the luteal phase (around 6–12 DPO) can sometimes align with implantation, when an embryo attaches to the uterine lining. Implantation spotting is usually very light, short-lived (hours to a day), and doesn’t turn into a regular period.
    How to tell the difference:
    • Timing: implantation happens earlier; pre-period spotting happens right before your period
    • Flow: implantation is very light; a period usually builds into flow
    • Duration: implantation is brief; a period continues
    
    If spotting is light and brief, it’s often not concerning. If it happens every cycle, lasts several days, or comes with pain or odor, it’s worth checking in with a doctor.

    Is an LH surge of 6.78 too low for conception?
----------------------------------------

[10:04 am] Coach:
    🌷 Why your LH surge looks lower 
    
    1️⃣ Ovulation depends on the rise, not the absolute number
    Inito identifies ovulation by detecting a rise from your personal baseline, not by reaching a universal threshold.
    LH levels starting from 6.5 to 101 mIU/ml can cause ovulation. Studies show that average LH levels during a surge are 44.6 mIU/ml.  https://www.researchgate.net/publication/272175944_Development_of_the_first_urinary_reproductive_hormone_ranges_referenced_to_independently_determined_ovulation_day
    
    2️⃣ Urinary LH levels vary widely
    LH in urine is influenced by:
    
    • hydration status
    • time of testing
    • metabolism & clearance rate
    • kidney filtration differences
    • baseline hormone physiology
    
    So even if you are ovulating at the same time every cycle, you can see very different numbers.
    
    3️⃣ Blood LH ≠ urine LH
    Urinary LH reflects what your body excretes, not the exact blood concentration.
    
    Some women produce a sharp, tall spike.
    Others produce a shorter or gentler surge that still triggers ovulation.
    
    4️⃣ Surge duration matters more than height
    A surge can be:
    
    • sharp & tall
    • short & narrow
    • gradual & modest
    
    Ovulation occurs if the brain-ovary signal is sufficient, regardless of peak height.
    
    What matters most:
    
    ✔ LH rise present
    ✔ PdG rise afterward
    
    Continue testing to look for a steady and consistent rise in PdG to confirm ovulation.

    Yes, some people do notice feeling a little light-headed or “floaty” around ovulation. 🌿
    
    🌼 Why this can happen
    Around ovulation, estrogen reaches its peak and then shifts rapidly. This hormonal transition can influence:
    • blood vessel dilation
    • blood pressure regulation
    • blood sugar balance
    • inner ear fluid balance
    
    That combination can create brief dizziness or light-headed sensations.
    
    🌸 Other ovulation-phase factors
    You may notice this more if you are: 
    • slightly dehydrated
    • skipping meals or low on iron/glucose
    • sensitive to hormonal shifts
    • experiencing ovulation pain (mittelschmerz)
