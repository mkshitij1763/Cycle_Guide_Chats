    Totally fair question — an early LH rise can feel unsettling, but there are a few very normal reasons this can happen. Let’s walk through what your chart is showing and why this isn’t automatically a problem. 🌱
    
    
    Why LH can surge early in a cycle
    
    1. Estrogen rose earlier than usual
    LH doesn’t act alone — it responds to estrogen.
    On your chart, E3G (estrogen) starts climbing early. When estrogen crosses your brain’s internal “threshold,” the brain releases LH.
    👉 Earlier estrogen rise = earlier LH signal.
    
    2. This may be a signal, not ovulation yet
    Early LH increases don’t always mean ovulation is happening immediately.
    Some cycles show:
    
    --> a preparatory LH rise
    --> a short or mild surge
    --> or even more than one LH rise before the ovulatory one
    
    Ovulation timing is confirmed only after a sustained PdG rise.
    
    3. Cycle-to-cycle variation
    Even in regular cycles, the follicular phase can shorten or lengthen.
    Things that can shift timing slightly:
    
    --> stress or illness in the previous cycle
    --> travel or sleep changes
    --> natural ovarian variability
    
    None of these imply anything is “off.”
    
    4. FSH + LH coordination
    FSH often moves alongside LH as follicles mature. An earlier follicle response can bring both hormones forward a bit — that’s physiology doing its thing, not a malfunction.
    
    What matters more than when LH rises
    
    --> Whether estrogen shows a clear build-up
    --> Whether PdG rises after the LH pattern
    --> Whether there is another LH surge
    
    Right now, your chart shows early signaling, not a conclusion.
