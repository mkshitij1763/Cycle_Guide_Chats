[05:59 am] Heather A:
    I had a ovarian cyst rupture on DPO 5. Am I out this month?
----------------------------------------

[08:33 am] Coach:
    A cyst rupture sounds dramatic, but it does not automatically close the door on this cycle. 🌿
    
    🌸 What likely happened
    After ovulation, the ovary forms the corpus luteum (the progesterone-producing structure). 
    It is common for the ovary to also have small functional cysts:
    Common luteal-phase cyst types
    
    👉🏻Corpus luteum cyst → the ovulated follicle fills with fluid/blood
    👉🏻Hemorrhagic cyst → small bleed inside a functional cyst
    
    These can occasionally rupture and cause:
    👉🏻sudden pelvic pain
    👉🏻spotting
    👉🏻temporary discomfort
    
    They often resolve on their own.
    
    🧭 Does a rupture at 5 DPO mean you’re out?
    Not necessarily.
    
    Pregnancy depends on: 
    ✔ egg release (already happened)
    ✔ fertilization (within ~24 hrs of ovulation)
    ✔ embryo travel (days 1–4)
    ✔ implantation (typically 6–12 DPO)
    
    At 5 DPO, implantation hasn’t happened yet.
    
    A rupture:
    ❌ does NOT “flush out” a fertilized egg
    ❌ does NOT stop implantation
    ❌ does NOT automatically reduce progesterone
    
    ✔ may still allow normal luteal function
    
    ⚠️ When it might affect the cycle
    Only if:
    👉🏻the corpus luteum is significantly damaged
    👉🏻progesterone drops prematurely
    👉🏻severe internal bleeding occurs (rare)
    👉🏻Most ruptures are self-limiting and fertility is preserved.
    
    🌼 What to watch now
    Seek medical care urgently if you experience:
    👉🏻severe or worsening pain
    👉🏻dizziness or fainting
    👉🏻shoulder pain (sign of internal bleeding)
    👉🏻heavy bleeding
    👉🏻fever
