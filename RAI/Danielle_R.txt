[02:37 pm] Danielle R:
    Your hormone values during CD 6–12 are typically considered baseline during the early follicular phase. 
    
    During the early follicular phase:
    
    👉🏻 Estrogen (E3G) is expected to be relatively low and steady before it begins its pre-ovulatory rise.
    👉🏻 LH typically stays at a low baseline without sharp spikes.
    👉🏻 PdG remains low because progesterone production from the ovary hasn’t started yet.
    👉🏻 FSH may show small fluctuations but usually stays modest.
    
    🌸 What matters more than “average numbers”
    
    Hormones are highly individual. Inito doesn’t compare your values to population averages—it looks at your unique baseline and then tracks how your hormones rise relative to that baseline.
    
    In your cycle, estrogen clearly rises after this baseline window, followed by an LH surge and a PdG rise confirming ovulation. That sequence tells us your baseline was appropriate for your body.
