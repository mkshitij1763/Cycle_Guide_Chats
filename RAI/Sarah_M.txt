--- SATURDAY, 07 MARCH 2026 ---

[12:03 am] Coach:
    At ~7 DPO, symptoms like cramps, nausea, fatigue, and even food aversions can happen in the luteal phase because progesterone is active after ovulation. Since progesterone rises in every ovulatory cycle, these symptoms alone can’t confirm pregnancy.
    
    Implantation usually happens around 6–12 DPO, and it takes a few more days for hCG to rise enough for a pregnancy test. So the only reliable way to know will be testing closer to 10–12 DPO or a day after your missed period.
    
    For now, try to stay hydrated, eat small meals if nausea is present, and rest if you’re feeling fatigued. If the abdominal pain becomes severe or persistent, it would be best to check in with a doctor.
    
    Just a quick note: Inito can track your fertile window and confirm ovulation by tracking hormone patterns, but it can’t be used to track or confirm pregnancy.
