[12:07 am] Annika P:
    Hi Annika
    
    • Progesterone is released in pulses.
    Progesterone from the corpus luteum doesn’t stay steady all day. It is released in bursts, and what shows up in urine depends on how much was metabolized and excreted at that moment. Because of this, day-to-day PdG fluctuations are very common. Inito measures PdG (urine metabolite of progesterone).
    
    • A mid-luteal estrogen rise is normal.
    It’s typical to see a small secondary estrogen increase in the mid-luteal phase. This is part of the natural luteal hormone pattern and helps support the uterine lining.
    
    • Single-day values are less important than the overall trend.
    What matters most is that ovulation was confirmed earlier with the PdG rise. After that, hormones can fluctuate quite a bit through the luteal phase.
    
    Also just a quick reminder: Inito can track your fertile window and confirm ovulation by tracking hormone patterns, but it can’t be used to track or confirm pregnancy.
